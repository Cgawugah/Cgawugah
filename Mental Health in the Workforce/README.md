**Breaking the Silence: A Data Driven Look at Mental Health in the Workplace**

This project presents a comprehensive multi page Power BI dashboard that analyzes global mental health trends in the workforce. It transforms raw survey data into structured insights on treatment patterns, workplace culture, stigma, remote work, and demographic differences across 46 countries.

The objective is to help organizations, HR teams, and policymakers better understand the factors that shape employee well being and to support the development of informed mental health strategies.

**Project Highlights**

• Six fully developed analytical dashboard pages
• Geographic analysis using map visuals
• AI driven insights through Key Influencers and Decomposition Tree
• Interactive filters and slicers for exploratory analysis
• Clean and consistent visual design
• Suitable for HR, wellness programs, policy research, and data driven decision making

**Dashboard Structure and Insights**
**1. Dashboard (Executive Summary)**

This page provides a consolidated view of the most important indicators including
• Respondent distribution across countries
• Company mental health benefits by organization size
• Relationship between treatment and remote work
• Comfort discussing mental health with coworkers
• Work interference patterns

This serves as the high level summary for leadership and stakeholders.

**2. Demographics Report**

This page outlines key characteristics of the dataset including
• Gender distribution
• Age groups and age bins
• Self employment status
• Remote work patterns
• Respondents by country
• Age distribution by gender
• Key Influencers analysis identifying factors that influence tech industry employment

This provides the foundation for understanding the population behind the insights.

**3. Treatment and Support Report**

This section focuses on access to and awareness of mental health support. It includes
• Family history and its relationship to seeking treatment
• Company size compared with mental health benefit availability
• Awareness of mental health benefits
• Country level patterns of work interference
• Frequency of mental health interference at work

This section reveals the conditions that encourage or discourage treatment uptake.

**4. Workplace Culture Report**

This page examines how workplace environment and communication influence mental health openness. It includes
• Comfort discussing mental health with supervisors
• Comfort discussing mental health with coworkers
• Comfort during interviews
• Care options provided by companies
• Benefits provided by companies

This section highlights the role that organizational culture plays in employee support.

**5. Remote Work and Company Size Report**

This page analyzes remote work patterns and how they relate to mental health. It includes
• Remote work rates by gender and country
• Work interference across companies
• Relationship between remote work and treatment
• Company size and support systems
• Interview support (physical and mental health)

This page connects workplace structure with well being and support availability.

**6. Consequences and Stigma Report**

This section provides deeper analytical insight into mental health outcomes. It includes
• Stigma rate
• Confidence in anonymity
• Mental and physical health consequences by country
• Observed consequences by company size
• Decomposition Tree for root cause analysis

This page delivers the most advanced analytical work using AI visuals.

**Tools and Technologies**

• Power BI Desktop for data modeling and visual design
• Power Query for data cleaning and preparation
• DAX for measures, KPIs and calculated insights
• AI visuals (Key Influencers and Decomposition Tree)
• Maps and geospatial visuals for country level insights

**Key Findings**

• Mental health benefits strongly increase the likelihood of seeking treatment
• Remote work affects work interference but does not significantly affect treatment decisions
• Men show lower treatment rates even when work interference is similar
• Larger companies generally offer more mental health support
• Comfort in discussing mental health is influenced by organizational culture
• Stigma and anonymity concerns remain major barriers to seeking help
